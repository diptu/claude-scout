# LLM Council System Summary

The LLM Council is a decision-making framework that routes questions through five independent AI advisors with distinct thinking styles, who then peer-review each other anonymously before a chairman synthesizes findings.

## The Five Advisors

1. **The Contrarian** — searches for fatal flaws and overlooked risks
2. **The First Principles Thinker** — questions underlying assumptions and reframes problems
3. **The Expansionist** — identifies upside potential and adjacent opportunities
4. **The Outsider** — provides fresh perspective without domain expertise
5. **The Executor** — focuses on practical implementation and immediate next steps

## When to Trigger

Use phrases like "council this," "run the council," or "should I X or Y" when facing decisions with genuine uncertainty and meaningful stakes. Avoid triggering on factual questions, simple tasks, or decisions without real tradeoffs.

## Process Flow

The methodology consists of five sequential steps:

1. **Frame the question** with workspace context enrichment to provide advisors substantive background
2. **Convene advisors** in parallel, each responding 150-300 words from their distinct angle
3. **Peer review** anonymously—advisors evaluate all five responses to identify strengths, blind spots, and collective gaps
4. **Chairman synthesis** producing a verdict covering agreements, disagreements, blind spots, recommendations, and one concrete next step
5. **Generate outputs**: an HTML report for scanning plus a markdown transcript for reference

## Key Principles

The framework emphasizes parallel processing over sequential, anonymization during review to prevent deference bias, and actionable recommendations rather than hedged advice. The chairman may override majority positions if reasoning supports it.
